Immediately after work on Friday, I rushed home to get ready for the Track the next day. I was only able to get in about 4 hours of sleep before having to wake up at 4 am. After getting everything into the car, I was on my way for the 160 mile journey up north to Willows, CA.<\/p>\n

Getting some gas for the beast:<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

I had to make a trip to a Walmart since places like Sports Authority weren’t open yet, and get some Under Armour type clothing. Surprisingly, there were a good amount of people at the Walmart even this early in the morning…<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Watching the sunrise somewhere on I-80E.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Eventually I made it there. It was still early morning and I could feel the temperatures starting to creep up.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Since this was my first Trackday ever, I took the Novice School and was in Group C. I also didn’t have time to prep my own bike, and felt it would be better to start with a 250 at the track. I rented one of these for the day:<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Fun little bike but absolutely no power. I would be flooring it at 11k on the straight and people would zoom past me like I wasn’t moving. Shifting on it wasn’t even in the same league as my Ducati. I also really disliked the upright riding position of this bike, felt like a scooter almost. It was, however, very light and very easy to throw around.<\/p>\n

These Pirelli’s were had a ton of grip and were very sticky\/tacky at the end of the day.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

At the end of the day, I probably drank close to 2 gallons of water. The temperatures were in the 100’s, and I had to be in a full leather suit… The most detrimental factor of the day was the fact that I was running on virtually no sleep, and with such a fatiguing activity I felt like a zombie the entire day. I had a little spur of energy after lunch, but I wish I was better rested. On the drive home, I was literally nodding off and falling asleep. I had to pull over in the middle of no where in order to get a nap in. Furthermore, having only been riding motorcycles for about a month, period, I was definitely at a handicap. Not to mention, the 250 that I rented was foreign to me, and it was the first time I rode a motorcycle in well over a week. Had to adapt quick, that’s for sure.<\/p>\n

All that aside, the whole experience was very humbling. I learned a lot and the practice was great. Most of the people there had experience, and there were a few sandbaggers in the C group. I remember getting passed by this girl that was leaned over dragging knee all the way down the righthander of the Hill.<\/p>\n

Very fun but extremely tiring and hot day. I’m definitely aiming to go back, hopefully with a bike of my own and a lot more rest.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
