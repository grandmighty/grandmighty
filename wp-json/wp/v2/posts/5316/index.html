While work is continued on the car, here is a mid-week update to tide things over. When ordering the MGW Short Shifter<\/a>, I opted for a threaded lever in a M12x1.75 rather than the regular slotted style lever for Camaros. The stock Mazda threading is a finer M10x1.25. The different lever threading meant I had to ditch the FEED shift knob<\/a>, unfortunately. On the bright side, this enabled me to try a new direction and go with a product that wasn’t quite off the shelf… I\u00a0looked around at various custom alternatives from companies and ended up shooting an email to Chris at WC Lathe Werks to see what he could do for me.<\/p>\n

After a few back and forth exchanges, I was able to finalize the exact shift knob that I visualized with him:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • 2″ diameter sphere<\/li>\n
  • 2.75″x1.2″ collar<\/li>\n
  • Blended transition<\/li>\n
  • 2 grip grooves<\/li>\n
  • Titanium<\/li>\n
  • Blue\/Bronze coloring<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    <\/a><\/p>\n

    I have always liked spherical shift knobs. With this being a hand made affair, I wanted to take the opportunity to elevate the design aspects. I decided on adding a taller collar to the shift knob to increase leverage and reachability. The quality and machine work on this shift knob is very high. I can’t wait to throw it on and start rowing through gears. I would personally rank a shift knob equal to a steering wheel in terms of “touch factor” and driver input importance.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
