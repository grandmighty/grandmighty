While it’s still technically Winter, with the way the weather has been this weekend, it might as well be Spring.<\/p>\n

The Garage has been home to many hours of work, but more or less forlorn in its own right. I decided to be productive, and divide some much needed attention to the garage itself.<\/p>\n

The main objective was to make everything more accessible and semi-organized. My\u00a0jurisdiction\u00a0lies only on the left side of the garage, so I used that to my advantage and moved unwanted items over to the right side. :-) After a bit of cleaning, relocating, and discarding – I was done. The garage still looked ghetto, but you gotta work with what you got.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

That brown, wooden shelf has a confused identity, since it was originally made to be in a kitchen.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

I hung my wrenches and an assortment of screwdrivers on the wall, which thereby alleviated space inside the tool cabinet.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

I’m definitely not one of those people that seemingly have every single tool possible in their garage, but I get by. I think just over the years of collecting and pilfering, I’ve amassed a reasonable inventory (also big ups to Sears and gift cards). To conclude, I went over my ratchet and socket set since they are so heavily used. All of the sockets were just stirred together in the top shelf of the tool cabinet. I cleaned out all the standard sockets since I’ve only ever used metric, which greatly made things more manageable.<\/p>\n

I probably should have done this a while ago, or at least paid more attention to the upkeep… Imagine the hours I could have saved if I didn’t stall from filtering through a hoard of junk before finding what I needed.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
